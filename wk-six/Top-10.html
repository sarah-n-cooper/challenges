Spring (Air France Remix)

Foxbase Beta

Saint Etienne

In a word, 1992. Air France respect Saint Etienne, and "Spring", too much to try to reinvent the wheel-- they're self-professed fans, and "Spring" is a song that "we fucked to when we were eleven and took rohypnol raw," so their revision is respectful to the source material in many ways. That is, except one-- in Air France's hands, the effervescent April showers of the original "Spring" are exchanged for a instantaneous full bloom. They don't necessarily rid the song of its alluring London fog (though they do forgo the song's relatively moody spoken-word bridges). Instead, Air France work around it, turning the drama in Sarah Cracknell's yearning ("Can't you see it's me who really loves you?") into something slightly more playful, and adding just enough percussive flourishes to give the song's beat a little more bounce. Essentially, Air France's makeover of this already gorgeous tune is a more palatable version of those obnoxious nerdy-turned-purty makeovers that features prominently in every coming-of-age drama. "Spring" didn't dress in frumpy sweaters, put its hair up in a bun, or wear (gasp!) glasses, but Air France know just how to cut the bangs and rouge the cheeks to make the song's beauty apparent to anyone within earshot.
